Close Encounters of the Third Kind

Intron014

Well... I finally gave into the Apple ecosystem after trying countless (as a matter of fact, 5) android tablets, none of which, were up for my school note-taking life. Nevertheless, when Apple announced in their Apple Event the 9th Generation of the iPad I thought to myself, "Why not?" And got it with a Logitech Crayon because... Honestly, I know that it has way less functionality than the Apple Pencil, but the shape... ANYWAYS! We're getting off track. Where were we... Oh yeah! The iPad.

From the moment I booted iPadOS 15 I fell in love with the simple yet incredibly powerful OS (And to be completely honest, I was blown away by the multitasking). To be clear, my goal buying the iPad was to have a device that I could take to school, get rid of my physical notebooks and books, and just... Carry it! Practically no weight at all.

As of writing this post, I've been using the iPad for a month and a bit. I've been using GoodNotes for my notetaking, as well as Quick Notes for when I have to jot something on the go (Perfect for when the teacher starts saying what's gonna be in the exam and you have nothing open). You might be wondering what has been of my previous notes on the physical notebooks, so the answer is... I scanned them all! I might not conserve the OCR but to be honest, I didn't have that either before.

On another note, shortcuts has been of great help! As you can see by the screenshot above, I have a very handy dandy Shortcut to add a new exam to my calendar following my usual naming scheme, perfection! (Also starring... Widgetsmith and Apollo!)

So yeah, in conclussion... This iPad is really (for what I've been able to tell for now) a great device!

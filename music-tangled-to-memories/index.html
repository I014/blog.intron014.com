Music Tangled To Memories

Intron014

Yesterday I was studying as usual, listening to random music that Spotify thought I may like when… The light in us all started playing. It honestly left me standing there, with just a thought (a very silly thought at that). Me playing Minecraft following ArticKraft’s videos on base building. Silly, I warned ya, nevertheless it left me thinking because I haven’t even glanced at that YouTuber since I was 12 or 13, and the fact that when the song played I immediately remembered the name of the YouTuber is wild. The same happens with, for example, the opening music from an Apple Event, I can recall precisely what event it was played and what they introduced at it.

This is an example of how music is really tangled into memories, I like to think of it as if it were the EXIF data (aka. Metadata) of a picture, except that with no aperture, camera and manufacturer’s data, but with the smells, music and good memories that can be retained from that moment. Again, this is wild. There is a chapter in the book called Springer Handbook of Systematic Musicology called Long-Term Memory for Music and it explains exceptionally well what I’m trying to convey over here… I might just be a Senior HS Student, therefore, just a mere opinion that has no weigh on anything, but I just like thinking about this stuff. Anyways, thanks for reading my silly thoughts, catch ya in the next one!
